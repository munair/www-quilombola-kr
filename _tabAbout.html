Munair Simpson is the sole proprietor of Quilombola, a business development and marketing strategy oriented operation serving customers in Asia, North America, and the Caribbean. For the past 17 years, Munair Simpson has been working in either an engineering or business development capacity for the mobile and new media industries in Asia and North America. In these roles, Munair has collaborated with business partners and served customers from all corners of the globe.

Munair Simpson

Prior to founding Quilombola, Munair was a senior manager at SK Telecom. While completing the requirements for the MBA at the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania, Munair was recruited to join SK's New Media Business Development Team. This engagement allowed him to make a smooth transition from the United States to Korea.

During his time at SK Telecom, Munair led the launch of SK's online business-to-business marketplace for audiovisual content after spending a summer working on strategy for the Mobile Network Operations (MNO) division. Munair spent over nine years in the United States fostering his passion for technology and new media startups by founding Network Resilience in 2001, and later by working for Gracenote, which was acquired by Sony.

Munair started his career in the engineering department at Cable and Wireless in the Cayman Islands after graduating from the University of the West Indies with a Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering. In his spare time, Munair enjoys teaching Capoeira and is presently trying to open another Capoeira school in Hong Kong.
